Independent Writing

Posted on 2019-09-30 In Writing strategy

准备托福，着重需要提高写作能力，这也是成为一个优秀的PhD Student 的必要条件。

首先说说托福写作的第一部分，这一部分相对简单，主要是从阅读材料和听力材料中找出作者的观点。首先判断两部分作者之间的态度，支持还是反对。一般听力材料都会针对阅读材料中每一个观点进行分析，阐述支持的理由或者反对的理由。这个Question中信息的收集难度不大，要提高成绩是在以信息的展示能力。在Response中我们的结构一般都是先说两部分材料作者的观点是相合还是相斥，然后逐条解释原因。在逐条解释中，一般都需要同时提到阅读和听力中的观点，这个时候，观点的组织能力和表达能力就很重要了。

然后我会从一些范文中开始积累观点表达的用法。

TPO 46

In the lecture, the professor casts doubt on the reading passage’s idea that storing patients’ medical records in electronic databases have several advantages over traditional paper-based record keeping. The professor asserts that the benefits are actually uncertain.

To begin with, according to the reading passage, electronic medical records will reduce costs of storing and transferring. The professor argue that the cost saving are unlikely as the reading suggests. She says that the doctor just keep the paper records as an emergency backup and most doctors who adopt electronic record keeping still have to pay storage cost associated with paper-based record keeping. On the top of that, the reading passage claims that the use of electronic medical records will help reduce the chances of medical errors. On the contrary, the professor rebuts that the electronic records cannot eliminate the possibility of errors. She says that doctors still use pen and paper while examining patients. It is usually the office staff of a doctor who will enter the information at a later time from the handwritten documents into electronic systems. So poor handwriting can still lead to errors. Lastly, the professor rebuts the reading’s point that electronic records will be beneficial to medical research through obtaining a great amount of data from patient records by stating that medical research would not necessarily benefit from record keeping. The professor points out that access to all medical information is subject to strict privacy laws in the US. Researchers who want to collect data from electronic medical records have to follow strict and complicated procedures and obtain many permissions including patient permission along the way. Often such permissions are not granted.
